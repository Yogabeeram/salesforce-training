# Salesforce CRM Basics

## 1. What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. It is a technology used by companies to manage interactions with customers, clients, and potential customers in an organized and efficient way.

CRM helps businesses:
- Store customer information
- Track communication
- Manage sales activities
- Improve customer relationships
- Increase business productivity

Instead of maintaining data manually in notebooks or spreadsheets, CRM systems provide a centralized platform where all customer-related information can be stored and accessed easily.

Salesforce is one of the most popular cloud-based CRM platforms used by companies worldwide.

---

# Benefits of CRM

- Stores customer data in one place
- Tracks sales and business opportunities
- Improves communication between teams
- Helps businesses provide better customer service
- Automates repetitive tasks
- Generates reports and analytics
- Improves customer satisfaction

CRM is important because maintaining strong customer relationships helps businesses grow successfully.

# 3. Important Salesforce Objects

## Account

An Account represents a company, organization, or business entity.

### Example:
- Infosys
- Amazon
- TCS

### Information Stored in Account
- Company Name
- Address
- Phone Number
- Industry Type

Accounts are used to store organization-related information.

---

## Contact

A Contact represents an individual person associated with an Account or organization.

### Example:
- HR Manager
- Project Manager
- Customer Representative

### Information Stored in Contact
- Name
- Email Address
- Phone Number
- Job Title

Contacts help businesses maintain proper communication with people related to organizations.

---

## Opportunity

An Opportunity represents a potential business deal or sales opportunity.

### Example:
- Software project deal
- Product purchase deal
- Service agreement

### Information Stored in Opportunity
- Opportunity Name
- Deal Value
- Sales Stage
- Expected Closing Date

Opportunities help companies track active business deals and revenue.

---

# 4. Business Workflow

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

## Explanation

### Lead
A person who shows interest in a service or product.

### Contact
After communication and verification, the person's details are stored as a contact.

### Opportunity
A possible business deal or process starts with the customer.

### Customer
After successful completion of the process, the person becomes a customer.

---

# Example Workflow

1. A student fills an admission inquiry form. (Lead)
2. After communication, the student details are verified. (Contact)
3. Admission process begins. (Opportunity)
4. After successful admission, the student becomes a customer.

# 5. Real-Life Mapping – College Admission System

| Salesforce Object | Real-World Example |
|---|---|
| Account | College |
| Contact | Student |
| Lead | Student Inquiry |
| Opportunity | Admission Process |

---

# Scenario Explanation

A college wants to manage student admissions using Salesforce CRM.

- The college is stored as an Account because it represents the organization.
- The student is stored as a Contact because the student interacts with the college.
- Student inquiry is stored as a Lead because the student shows interest in admission.
- Admission process is stored as an Opportunity because it represents a possible admission.

Using this structure, the college can:
- Manage student information
- Track admission inquiries
- Monitor admission processes
- Improve communication efficiently
