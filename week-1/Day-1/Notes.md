# Salesforce Summer Program – Day 1

## Introduction

Salesforce is a cloud-based Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform that helps organizations manage customer interactions, sales, services, and business processes efficiently.

It provides businesses with tools to store customer information, track sales activities, automate workflows, and improve communication between teams and customers.

Salesforce is widely used because it is scalable, secure, and supports both administrators and developers in building business solutions.

# What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.

CRM is a system used by companies to:
- Manage customer information
- Track customer interactions
- Improve sales and support
- Increase customer satisfaction
- Organize business workflows

CRM helps companies maintain strong relationships with customers and improve business growth.

---

# Why Companies Use Salesforce

Companies use Salesforce because it:
- Stores customer data securely
- Improves sales management
- Automates business processes
- Enhances customer support
- Provides cloud accessibility
- Generates reports and analytics
- Improves team collaboration

Salesforce helps businesses save time and improve productivity.

# Salesforce Core Concepts

## Account

An Account represents a company or organization.

### Example:
- Infosys
- TCS
- Amazon

Accounts are used to store company-related information.

---

## Contact

A Contact represents a person associated with an account.

### Example:
- HR Manager
- Customer
- Student

Contacts store individual details such as:
- Name
- Phone Number
- Email

---

## Opportunity

An Opportunity represents a potential business deal or sales process.

### Example:
- Software purchase deal
- Admission process
- Hospital treatment package

Opportunities help companies track revenue and sales progress.

---

# Business Workflow

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

## Explanation

### Lead
A person who shows interest in a product or service.

### Contact
After verification, the lead becomes a contact.

### Opportunity
A possible business deal is created.

### Customer
When the deal is successful, the person becomes a customer.

This workflow helps organizations manage sales and customer relationships effectively.

# Real-Life Mapping – College Admission System

| Salesforce Term | Real-Life Example |
|---|---|
| Account | College |
| Contact | Student |
| Lead | Student Inquiry |
| Opportunity | Admission Process |

## Workflow Explanation

1. A student inquires about admission.
2. Student details are stored as a lead.
3. After communication, the student becomes a contact.
4. Admission process becomes an opportunity.
5. After successful admission, the student becomes a customer of the institution.
